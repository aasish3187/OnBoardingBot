CHAPTER 3: CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

All employees of Nexus Technologies are expected to maintain the highest
standards of professional conduct. This includes:

PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR:
- Treat all colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect and dignity
- Maintain confidentiality of proprietary information and client data
- Avoid conflicts of interest and disclose any potential conflicts to your
  manager and HR immediately
- Use company resources responsibly and only for business purposes
- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and company policies

WORKPLACE COMMUNICATION:
- Use professional language in all communications (email, Slack, meetings)
- Be responsive to messages within 4 business hours
- Keep meetings focused and time-bound; always share an agenda beforehand
- Practice active listening and provide constructive feedback

SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY:
- Do not share confidential company information on social media
- Clearly state that personal views do not represent the company
- Do not engage in online debates that could harm the company's reputation
- Seek approval from the Communications team before posting about the company

CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATIONS:
Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action, ranging
from a verbal warning to termination, depending on the severity. Repeated
minor violations will be escalated progressively.

CHAPTER 4: WORKPLACE ETHICS & ANTI-HARASSMENT POLICY
================================================================================

Nexus Technologies is committed to providing a safe, inclusive, and
harassment-free workplace for everyone, regardless of gender, race, religion,
age, disability, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic.

ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY:
We maintain a strict zero-tolerance policy against:
- Sexual harassment (verbal, physical, or visual)
- Bullying, intimidation, or threats
- Discrimination in hiring, promotion, or compensation
- Retaliation against anyone who reports misconduct

REPORTING PROCEDURES:
If you experience or witness harassment or unethical behavior:
1. Report to your immediate manager (if they are not the offender)
2. Contact HR directly at ethics@nexustech.com
3. Use the anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1800-555-ETHICS (1800-555-38442)
4. File a formal complaint through the HR portal under "Ethics & Compliance"

All reports are investigated confidentially within 10 business days. The
investigating committee includes HR representatives, a legal advisor, and
an independent external member for serious cases.

WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION:
Employees who report misconduct in good faith are fully protected under our
Whistleblower Protection Policy. Retaliation of any kind is strictly
prohibited and is itself grounds for termination.

CHAPTER 5: DRESS CODE POLICY
================================================================================

Nexus Technologies follows a SMART CASUAL dress code for regular workdays.
We believe in a comfortable yet professional appearance.

GENERAL GUIDELINES:
- Smart casual attire is expected Monday through Thursday
- Casual Fridays allow jeans, t-shirts, and sneakers
- Business formal is required for client meetings and presentations
- All clothing should be clean, neat, and in good condition

ACCEPTABLE ATTIRE:
- Collared shirts, polo shirts, blouses
- Chinos, tailored trousers, dress pants
- Closed-toe shoes, loafers, clean sneakers (Fridays only)
- Blazers and jackets (optional)
- Traditional/ethnic wear is welcome and encouraged

NOT ACCEPTABLE:
- Torn or ripped clothing
- Offensive graphics or slogans on clothing
- Beachwear, flip-flops, or athletic wear (except on Sports Day)
- Excessively revealing clothing

REMOTE WORK DRESS CODE:
For virtual meetings, smart casual from the waist up is expected. Please
ensure your background is professional or use a virtual background.

EXCEPTIONS:
- Lab and server room staff must wear company-provided safety gear
- Special dress code events (cultural days, team outings) will be
  communicated in advance

STANDARD WORKING HOURS:
- Regular hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Monday to Friday)

- All employees must log attendance via the HRMS portal or biometric system
- Remote employees must check in on Slack (#attendance channel) by 10:00 AM

OVERTIME POLICY:
- Overtime is applicable only for non-exempt employees
- Prior approval from your manager is required for overtime work
- Overtime compensation: 1.5x regular hourly rate for weekdays, 2x for
  weekends and holidays
- Managers should ensure overtime does not exceed 10 hours per week

WORK FROM HOME (WFH):
- All employees are eligible for up to 2 WFH days per week
- WFH requests must be submitted 24 hours in advance via HRMS
- Wednesdays are mandatory in-office days for all teams
- New employees must complete 90 days before availing WFH

CHAPTER 7: PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS
================================================================================

Nexus Technologies follows a comprehensive performance management system
designed to support employee growth and development.

REVIEW CYCLE:
- Annual Performance Review: Conducted in March-April each year
- Mid-Year Check-in: Conducted in September-October
- Quarterly 1-on-1s: Between employee and direct manager
- 360-Degree Feedback: Collected annually from peers, reports, and managers

REVIEW PROCESS:
1. GOAL SETTING (April): Employees set 4-6 SMART goals with their manager
   at the start of each fiscal year
2. SELF-ASSESSMENT: Employees complete a self-assessment form rating their
   performance against goals
3. MANAGER ASSESSMENT: Managers provide ratings and detailed feedback
4. CALIBRATION: Department heads calibrate ratings across teams for fairness
5. REVIEW MEETING: Manager and employee discuss ratings, feedback, and
   development plan in a 60-minute face-to-face meeting
6. FINAL RATING: Published on HRMS within 2 weeks of review meetings

RATING SCALE:
- 5 (Exceptional): Consistently exceeds all expectations
- 4 (Exceeds Expectations): Frequently goes above and beyond
- 3 (Meets Expectations): Consistently meets job requirements
- 2 (Needs Improvement): Partially meets expectations
- 1 (Unsatisfactory): Fails to meet minimum standards

IMPACT OF PERFORMANCE RATINGS:
- Ratings directly influence annual salary increments (typically 8-20%)
- Bonus payouts are tied to individual and company performance
- Promotion eligibility requires a rating of 4 or above for 2 consecutive years
- Employees rated 2 or below are placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

CHAPTER 9: GRIEVANCE & COMPLAINT PROCEDURES
================================================================================

Nexus Technologies encourages open communication and provides multiple
channels for employees to raise concerns, grievances, or complaints.

INFORMAL RESOLUTION:
1. Discuss the concern directly with the person involved (if comfortable)
2. Speak with your immediate manager
3. Consult your HR Business Partner for guidance

FORMAL GRIEVANCE PROCESS:
1. Submit a written grievance through the HRMS portal ("Grievance" section)
2. Acknowledgment received within 2 business days
3. Investigation conducted by HR within 10 business days
4. Resolution communicated to the employee with action plan
5. Appeal process available if the employee is unsatisfied with the resolution

GRIEVANCE COMMITTEE:
- Comprises HR Head, Legal Counsel, and 2 elected employee representatives
- Meets monthly to review open grievances
- Ensures fair and unbiased resolution

CONFIDENTIALITY:
All grievances are handled with strict confidentiality. Only individuals
directly involved in the investigation will have access to the details.

CHAPTER 10: SEPARATION & EXIT POLICY
================================================================================

NOTICE PERIOD:
- Employees at levels L1-L4: 30 days notice period
- Employees at levels L5 and above: 60 days notice period
- Notice period can be waived at the company's discretion
- Notice period buy-out is available (employee pays basic salary for
  remaining days)

RESIGNATION PROCESS:
1. Submit resignation through HRMS portal
2. Manager conducts retention discussion within 3 business days
3. HR schedules exit interview
4. Knowledge transfer to be completed during notice period
5. Return all company assets (laptop, ID card, access cards)
6. Full and final settlement within 45 days of last working day

EXIT INTERVIEW:
- Conducted by HR (not the reporting manager) to ensure candid feedback
- Feedback is used to improve workplace policies and culture
- All exit interview data is anonymized and aggregated

REHIRING POLICY:
- Former employees may apply for open positions after 6 months
- Rehired employees start with a fresh probation period
- Previous tenure is not counted for benefits unless specified
