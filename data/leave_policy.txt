CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION & SCOPE
================================================================================

This Leave & Attendance Policy applies to all full-time employees of Nexus
Technologies Pvt. Ltd., effective from January 1, 2025. Contract employees
and interns have separate leave policies communicated at the time of joining.

The objective of this policy is to:
- Provide clear guidelines on leave entitlements and procedures
- Ensure fair and consistent application of leave rules across the organization
- Support work-life balance while maintaining business continuity
- Comply with applicable labor laws and regulations

This policy supersedes all previous versions of the leave policy. In case
of any conflict between this policy and applicable law, the law shall prevail.

CHAPTER 2: TYPES OF LEAVE
================================================================================

Nexus Technologies provides the following categories of leave to all
eligible full-time employees:

2.1 CASUAL LEAVE (CL)
- Entitlement: 12 days per calendar year
- Purpose: Personal errands, family events, minor illness, emergencies
- Maximum consecutive days: 3 days
- Can be taken as half-day (first half: 9 AM - 1 PM, second half: 2 PM - 6 PM)
- Not applicable during probation period (first 90 days)
- Cannot be combined with other leave types (except public holidays)

2.2 SICK LEAVE (SL)
- Entitlement: 12 days per calendar year
- Purpose: Employee illness, medical appointments, recovery
- Medical certificate required for sick leave exceeding 2 consecutive days
- Can be taken without prior approval (inform manager by 9:30 AM)
- Available from Day 1 of employment
- Cannot be used for family member's illness (use Casual Leave)

2.3 EARNED LEAVE / PRIVILEGE LEAVE (EL/PL)
- Entitlement: 15 days per calendar year
- Purpose: Planned vacations, extended personal time off
- Accrual: 1.25 days per month, available after completion of probation
- Must be applied at least 7 days in advance for 1-3 day requests
- Must be applied at least 15 days in advance for requests of 4+ days
- Maximum consecutive days: 10 days (exceptions approved by Department Head)
- Can be combined with public holidays and weekends

2.4 COMPENSATORY OFF (COMP OFF)
- Earned when an employee works on a public holiday or weekend
- Must be availed within 30 days of earning
- Prior approval from manager required for weekend/holiday work
- Maximum accumulation: 3 comp-offs at any time
- Expires if not used within the 30-day window

2.5 MATERNITY LEAVE
- Entitlement: 26 weeks (182 days) for first two children
- 12 weeks (84 days) for third child onwards
- Applicable to female employees who have worked for at least 80 days
  in the 12 months preceding the expected delivery date
- Fully paid leave as per the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017
- Can be availed up to 8 weeks before the expected delivery date
- Includes prenatal and postnatal care
- Work-from-home option available for 4 weeks after returning from
  maternity leave

2.6 PATERNITY LEAVE
- Entitlement: 15 days
- Applicable within 6 months of the child's birth or adoption
- Can be taken in a single stretch or in two parts
- Fully paid leave
- Available for up to 2 children

2.7 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
- Entitlement: 5 days for immediate family (spouse, children, parents,
  siblings)
- 3 days for extended family (grandparents, in-laws, close relatives)
- No advance notice required — inform manager as soon as possible
- Fully paid leave

2.8 MARRIAGE LEAVE
- Entitlement: 5 days
- Available once during employment tenure
- Must be availed within 1 month of the marriage date
- Marriage invitation or certificate to be submitted to HR

ACCRUAL RULES:

  joiners, leaves are pro-rated based on the month of joining.
- Sick Leave: Credited in full on January 1st each year. Pro-rated for

CHAPTER 4: LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS
================================================================================

All leave requests must be submitted through the HRMS portal. Here is the
step-by-step process:

STEP 1: LOG IN TO HRMS
- Go to https://hrms.nexustech.com
- Log in with your company credentials

STEP 2: SUBMIT LEAVE REQUEST
- Navigate to: My Leaves > Apply Leave
- Select the leave type (CL, SL, EL, Comp Off, etc.)
- Choose the start date and end date
- Select Full Day or Half Day
- Enter the reason for leave (mandatory)
- Attach supporting documents if required (medical certificate for SL > 2 days)
- Click "Submit"

STEP 3: NOTIFICATION
- Your manager receives an email and Slack notification
- You receive a confirmation email with the leave request ID
- Status shows as "Pending Approval" in the portal

STEP 4: TRACK STATUS
- My Leaves > Leave History shows all your leave requests
- Status: Pending / Approved / Rejected / Cancelled

CANCELLATION:
- You can cancel a pending or approved leave request before the start date
- To cancel: My Leaves > Leave History > Select Request > Cancel
- Cancelled leaves are credited back to your balance within 1 business day

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Leave applications should be submitted before 12:00 PM of the leave start
  date (for unplanned leaves like sick leave)
- Late applications may be marked as "Leave Without Pay" (LWP) at the
  manager's discretion
- For emergencies where you cannot access HRMS, inform your manager via
  call/SMS and apply on HRMS within 2 days of returning

CHAPTER 5: MANAGER APPROVAL WORKFLOW
================================================================================

The leave approval process follows a structured workflow:

APPROVAL HIERARCHY:
1. Leave requests of 1-3 days: Approved by immediate manager (L+1)
2. Leave requests of 4-7 days: Approved by manager + Department Head (L+2)
3. Leave requests of 8+ days: Approved by manager + Department Head + HR

APPROVAL TIMELINE:
- Managers must act on leave requests within 2 business days
- If no action is taken within 3 business days, the request auto-escalates
  to the next level
- Urgent/emergency leave requests should be approved within 4 hours

GROUNDS FOR REJECTION:
Managers may reject leave requests for the following reasons:
- Critical project deadlines or deliverables
- Insufficient team coverage (minimum 50% team attendance required)
- Leave blackout period (e.g., quarter-end, audit periods)
- Incomplete leave application (missing reason or documents)

REJECTION PROCESS:
- Manager must provide a reason for rejection
- Employee receives email notification with the rejection reason
- Employee can resubmit with modified dates or escalate to HR

LEAVE BLACKOUT PERIODS:
The following periods are subject to leave restrictions:
- Last week of each financial quarter (March, June, September, December)
- Annual audit period (April 1-15)
- Company-wide events (annual conference, town halls)
- Managers may declare team-specific blackout periods with 2 weeks' notice

CHAPTER 7: LEAVE CARRY-FORWARD & ENCASHMENT RULES
================================================================================

CARRY-FORWARD RULES:
- Casual Leave: NOT carried forward. Unused CL lapses on December 31st.
- Sick Leave: NOT carried forward. Unused SL lapses on December 31st.
- Earned Leave: Can be carried forward to the next year, subject to a
  maximum accumulation cap of 30 days.
- Comp Off: NOT carried forward. Must be used within 30 days of earning.

LEAVE ENCASHMENT:
- Only Earned Leave (EL) is eligible for encashment
- Encashment is calculated at: (Basic Salary / 30) × Number of EL days
- Encashment is available at the following times:
  1. At the time of separation/resignation (all accumulated EL)
  2. Once per year in December (for EL balance exceeding 15 days, you may
     encash the excess)
  3. At retirement (all accumulated EL)

LEAVE LAPSE:
- Casual Leave and Sick Leave balance resets to zero on January 1st
- Earned Leave exceeding 30 days is auto-encashed in January payroll
- Employees are encouraged to use their leave entitlements — rest is
  important for productivity and well-being!

EXAMPLES:
1. Employee A has 5 unused CL and 20 EL on Dec 31:
   → CL (5 days): Lapses
   → EL (20 days): Carries forward fully (under 30-day cap)

2. Employee B has 35 EL on Dec 31:
   → EL (30 days): Carries forward
   → EL (5 days excess): Auto-encashed in January payroll

CHAPTER 8: SPECIAL LEAVE PROVISIONS
================================================================================

8.1 SABBATICAL LEAVE
- Eligibility: Employees with 5+ years of continuous service
- Duration: 1 to 3 months (unpaid, but with job protection)
- Purpose: Higher education, personal projects, caregiving, travel
- Application: Submit at least 2 months in advance to HR and Department Head
- Benefits during sabbatical: Health insurance continues; ESOPs vest normally

8.2 ADOPTION LEAVE
- Female employees: Same as maternity leave (26 weeks for first two children)
- Male employees: 15 days (same as paternity leave)
- Must provide adoption papers to HR

8.3 MISCARRIAGE / MEDICAL TERMINATION LEAVE
- Entitlement: 6 weeks from the date of miscarriage or medical termination
- Medical certificate required
- Fully paid leave
- Confidentiality strictly maintained

8.4 JURY DUTY / GOVERNMENT SUMMONS
- Paid leave for the duration of the duty
- Submit official summons to HR
- Resume work within 1 day of duty completion

8.5 ELECTION LEAVE
- Paid leave on the day of state or national elections
- No application required — auto-declared by HR if office is in the
  election constituency

8.6 NATURAL DISASTER / PANDEMIC LEAVE
- Special leave may be declared by the CEO during natural disasters,
  pandemics, or other force majeure events
- Terms will be communicated via company-wide email and Slack #general

CHAPTER 9: UNAUTHORIZED ABSENCE & CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Unauthorized absence is when an employee is absent from work without:
- Submitting a leave application
- Informing their manager
- Having approved leave in the system

CONSEQUENCES:
1. FIRST OCCURRENCE:
   - Verbal warning from the manager
   - Leave marked as "Leave Without Pay" (LWP)
   - Deduction from salary: (Basic Salary / 30) × LWP days

2. SECOND OCCURRENCE (within 6 months):
   - Written warning issued by HR
   - Leave marked as LWP
   - Incident recorded in personnel file

3. THIRD OCCURRENCE (within 12 months):
   - Final written warning
   - May affect performance review rating
   - Mandatory counseling session with HR

4. REPEATED VIOLATIONS:
   - Considered gross misconduct
   - May lead to termination of employment

EXCEPTIONS:
- Medical emergencies where the employee was unable to communicate
  (hospitalization, accident) — medical proof required
- Natural disasters or situations beyond the employee's control

CHAPTER 10: ATTENDANCE & TIME TRACKING
================================================================================

ATTENDANCE SYSTEM:
- Office employees: Biometric attendance (fingerprint or face recognition)
  at building entrances
- Remote employees: Self-declaration via HRMS portal + Slack #attendance
- Hybrid employees: Biometric on office days, self-declaration on WFH days

MINIMUM ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENTS:
- All employees must maintain a minimum attendance of 90% per month
  (excluding approved leaves)
- Employees below 85% attendance for 2 consecutive months will receive
  an attendance warning from HR
- Chronic low attendance may trigger a review meeting with HR

LATE ARRIVAL POLICY:
- Grace period: 15 minutes (arrival by 10:15 AM if core hours start at 10 AM)
- More than 3 late arrivals in a month: 0.5 CL deducted automatically
- Consistently late employees will be counseled by their manager

EARLY DEPARTURE:
- Leaving before 4:00 PM (end of core hours) requires manager approval
- Unapproved early departures are marked as half-day absence

ATTENDANCE REGULARIZATION:
If you forget to mark attendance:
1. Go to HRMS > Attendance > Regularization Request
2. Select the date and provide a reason
3. Your manager will approve/reject within 1 business day
4. Regularization requests must be submitted within 3 days
